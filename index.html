Final Project
Evaluation of SIRAH - a Coarse-Grained Protein Model; the Validity of the Forcefield in the context of Partition Coefficients

Coarse graining is a method for developing a forcefield to model systems in molecular dynamics, and SIRAH is an example of such a forcefield. In this study the validity of this forcefield was investigated for use on modelling proteins by carrying out free energy calculations to determine the octanol - water partition coefficients of amino acid side chains. These were obtained for two different models of octanol, developed using the SIRAH parameters and optimized using ForceBalance. The results for both models showed that currently the SIRAH forcefield could not be validated for use in modelling proteins.
